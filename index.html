About Albert Camus

Albert Camus (1913-1960) was a French-Algerian philosopher, author, and playwright known for his contributions to existentialism and absurdism, two philosophical movements that explore the human condition and the meaning of life. Camus was born in French Algeria and later moved to France, where he became associated with a group of writers and thinkers who explored the existentialist philosophy, including Jean-Paul Sartre.

Despite his connection to existentialism, Camus' works also exhibit elements of absurdism, a philosophy that suggests that the human search for meaning in an inherently meaningless and indifferent universe is ultimately futile.
